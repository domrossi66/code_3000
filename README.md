## Ethics Statement on AI

I believe ethics statements should be more than a simple declaration. They need to contain a human element to them that makes them feel ethical rather than corporate buzzwords thrown together. 

Ethics statements should clearly define the task they aim to accomplish. Broad, generic claims like “making life better” fail to provide meaningful direction. Instead, ethical statements should be include the work being done, why it matters, and who is involved. This level of specificity ensures that ethical considerations are directly tied to real-world applications rather than abstract ideals.

These statements should also have a sense of empathy and purpose. Ethics are a measure to make sure people are treated with some fairness and their voices are heard. When those human elements are not included in an ethics statement it fails to be ethical because it is not protecting the interests of people. It is just a lazy statement that makes no difference to anyone.

Ultimately, I see ethics statements as tools for accountability. They should guide action, clarify intent, and reflect a genuine commitment to responsible and thoughtful work.
